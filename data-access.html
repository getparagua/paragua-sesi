Data Access

v0.1: benchmark artifacts (no raw dataset)

SESI is published as a visual market benchmark and a small set of public benchmark artifacts. It is not distributed as a full historical dataset in v0.1.

What is public in v0.1?

v0.1 is intentionally lightweight. We publish a small set of artifacts that make SESI easy to read and easy to reference without turning it into a raw data dump.

These artifacts are meant for interpretation and communication. They do not provide full time-series data.

  • Today snapshot (JSON) — current SESI, structural context, expected stress (OMIP), dislocation, and a human-readable note.
    File: assets/public/today.json
  • Validation summary (JSON) — a small “what usually happens next” table by regime (medians + sample sizes).
    File: assets/public/validation_summary.json
  • Charts (HTML) — canonical visual views for stress, dislocation, and validation.
    Files: assets/charts/stress.html, assets/charts/dislocation.html, assets/charts/validation.html
  • Optional: regime table (CSV, aggregated) — a small summary table (not a time series) used to support the validation chart.
    File: assets/public/quadrants_summary.csv (if published)

What is NOT public in v0.1?

SESI v0.1 does not provide:

  • full historical SESI time series for download
  • raw OMIE / OMIP curated datasets
  • an API or real-time feed

This is a deliberate product choice: SESI is designed as a benchmark and communication tool, not as a raw market data distribution channel.

Future availability

Historical datasets, higher-frequency updates, and programmatic access (API) may be introduced in a future release. Extended access may be granted selectively for research or institutional use.

If you are interested in early access, please get in touch.
