Methodology

High-level overview (v0.1, frozen)

Concept

SESI (Spain Energy Stress Index) measures stress regimes in the Spanish power market by combining three complementary dimensions:

  1. Price level — distance from long-term historical baselines
  2. Short-term tension — unusual recent price movements
  3. Market turbulence — intensity of recent volatility

These dimensions are aggregated into a daily stress signal and mapped into percentile space for intuitive interpretation.

Percentile interpretation

SESI is expressed as a percentile relative to its own historical distribution.

A percentile of p indicates that the current level of stress is lower than approximately (100 − p)% of days in the reference period.

Percentiles represent historical ranking, not probabilities.

Realized vs structural vs expected stress

  • SESI: realized (spot) market stress, updated daily
  • SESI_structural: distance from the pre-2022 structural regime
  • Expected stress: forward-looking expectations derived from OMIP futures

Comparing these dimensions allows the identification of dislocations between current stress conditions and how the market prices future risk.

What SESI is — and what it is not

  • SESI is a market regime indicator
  • SESI is designed for risk communication and monitoring
  • SESI is not a trading signal
  • SESI is not a probabilistic forecast

Data sources

SESI is built exclusively using publicly available market data:

  • OMIE — Spanish day-ahead power market marginal prices
  • OMIP — Iberian power futures settlement prices (Month contracts)
  • Yahoo Finance — continuous TTF natural gas futures (TTF=F)

No proprietary, internal, or confidential data sources are used.
